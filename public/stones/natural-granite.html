What Is Natural Granite?

Granite is an igneous rock formed deep beneath the earth's surface over millions of years. Composed primarily of quartz, feldspar, and mica, each slab carries a completely unique pattern of minerals, crystals, and veining that can never be replicated.

One-of-a-Kind Beauty

No two granite slabs are identical. The natural variation in color, movement, and crystal structure means your countertop is truly yours alone.

Extreme Heat Resistance

Set a hot pot directly on granite without worry. It can withstand temperatures well above what your kitchen produces.

Proven Durability

Granite has been used in architecture for thousands of years. With proper sealing, it easily lasts 25+ years in your home.

Adds Home Value

Natural stone countertops consistently rank as a top feature buyers look for, offering strong return on investment.

Care & Maintenance

The most important thing: Seal your granite countertops once a year. This 15-minute task is the key to keeping granite looking beautiful for decades.

Daily Cleaning

Use a stone-specific cleaner or a few drops of pH-neutral dish soap with warm water. Wipe with a soft cloth and dry. Avoid paper towels on polished surfaces — they can leave micro-scratches over time.

What to Avoid

  • Acidic cleaners: Vinegar, lemon juice, and citrus-based cleaners can etch the surface
  • Ammonia or bleach: These strip the sealer and can discolor the stone
  • Leaving spills: Blot (don't wipe) spills immediately — especially wine, coffee, and oil

Why Sealing Matters

Granite is a natural stone with microscopic pores. Sealing fills these pores, creating a barrier against stains and moisture. A properly sealed granite countertop is remarkably resistant to everyday spills.

We can help: Countertop World offers guidance on the right sealers and maintenance schedule for your specific granite variety.

Cost Guide

Natural granite has one of the widest price ranges: $40 to $200+ per square foot installed. The massive variation comes from the stone's origin, rarity, and visual character.

What Affects the Price

Origin & Rarity

Common domestic granites are affordable. Exotic imported varieties from Brazil, India, or Italy command premium prices.

Color & Pattern

Whites and blues tend to cost more than browns and tans. Dramatic movement and unique mineral formations increase price.

Thickness & Finish

3cm slabs cost more than 2cm. Leathered and honed finishes require extra processing versus standard polished.

Is Natural Granite Right for You?

Perfect For You If…

  • You love the unique character of natural stone
  • You're a cook who wants to set hot pans down freely
  • You're building an outdoor kitchen
  • You appreciate that no one else will have your exact countertop
  • You want proven, time-tested durability

Consider It If…

  • You're torn between granite and quartz (visit us to compare side-by-side)
  • You want natural stone on a moderate budget
  • You're open to annual sealing as part of home care

Look Elsewhere If…

  • You want absolutely zero maintenance (consider quartz)
  • You need perfectly consistent patterns across a large project
  • You prefer a perfectly uniform, man-made aesthetic

Lifestyle match: Granite is for homeowners who appreciate nature's artistry and don't mind 15 minutes of annual care in exchange for a surface with genuine, irreplaceable character.
