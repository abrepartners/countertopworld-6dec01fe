Non-porous, low maintenance, and endlessly versatile. The modern standard for kitchens and bathrooms across Arkansas.

What Is Engineered Quartz?

Engineered quartz is manufactured from approximately 90-95% ground natural quartz crystals bound together with polymer resins, pigments, and recycled materials. The result is one of the most durable and versatile countertop surfaces available today.

Non-Porous Surface

Never needs sealing. Resists bacteria, mold, and staining without any special treatment.

Consistent Patterns

Unlike natural stone, quartz delivers uniform color and pattern across every slab. What you see in the showroom is what you get.

Wide Color Range

From marble-look veining to solid whites, concrete tones, and bold colors — hundreds of options from top brands.

Exceptional Durability

Scratch-resistant, chip-resistant, and built to handle the demands of busy households for 25+ years.

Care & Maintenance

The short version: Engineered quartz is one of the lowest-maintenance countertop surfaces you can buy. A little soap and water is all you need for daily care.

Daily Cleaning

Wipe surfaces with a soft cloth, warm water, and a few drops of mild dish soap. Dry with a clean towel. That's it — really.

What to Avoid

  • Harsh chemicals: Bleach, oven cleaners, and drain openers can damage the resin binders
  • Abrasive pads: Steel wool and scouring pads will dull the finish over time
  • Direct heat: Always use trivets or hot pads — sustained heat above 300°F can discolor the resin

Stain Prevention

While quartz is highly stain-resistant, it's best to wipe up spills (especially wine, coffee, or turmeric) promptly. For dried-on residue, a non-abrasive cleaner or a paste of baking soda and water works well.

Sealing? Never needed. Engineered quartz is non-porous right out of the box and stays that way for life.

Cost Guide

Engineered quartz typically ranges from $50 to $150 per square foot installed, depending on the brand, color, edge profile, and project complexity.

What Affects the Price

Brand & Color

Premium brands (Cambria) and exotic patterns cost more. Solid colors and simpler designs are budget-friendly.

Edge Profile

Simple eased edges are included. Waterfall, mitered, and ogee edges add $10-$30/linear foot.

Project Complexity

Sink cutouts, cooktop openings, seam placement, and backsplash integration affect the final cost.

Is Engineered Quartz Right for You?

Perfect For You If…

  • You want a beautiful surface without ongoing maintenance
  • You have a busy household with kids
  • You prefer consistent patterns and wide color choices
  • You're building rental or investment properties
  • You value a surface that never needs sealing

Consider It If…

  • You want luxury without marble's upkeep
  • You're choosing between quartz and granite
  • You need outdoor surfaces (check UV-stable options)

Look Elsewhere If…

  • You want 100% natural stone character
  • You routinely set hot pans directly on surfaces
  • You love the idea of a surface that develops patina

Lifestyle match: Engineered quartz is the "set it and forget it" surface. If you want to spend time enjoying your kitchen instead of maintaining it, quartz is likely your best choice.
