From soapstone's velvety warmth to onyx's translucent drama. Statement pieces for homeowners who dare to be different.

Two Extraordinary Stones

Soapstone and onyx represent the far ends of the countertop spectrum — one warm, forgiving, and understated; the other dramatic, translucent, and bold. Both are exotic choices that make a statement no other material can match.

Soapstone: Velvety Warmth

A naturally non-porous metamorphic rock with a soft, velvety texture. Used in science labs for centuries, soapstone is remarkably resistant to chemicals and heat. It darkens beautifully over time — a living surface.

Onyx: Translucent Drama

A calcite-based stone with stunning translucency. When backlit, onyx glows from within — creating one of the most visually striking effects in all of interior design.

Soapstone Character

Starts as light gray and gradually darkens to a rich charcoal. You can accelerate this with mineral oil or let it happen naturally. Either way, the result is stunning.

Onyx Rarity

Onyx is one of the rarest countertop materials. Each slab features dramatic, flowing veining and colors ranging from creamy white to deep green, honey gold, and pink.

Care & Maintenance

Soapstone Care

Surprisingly forgiving. Soapstone is naturally non-porous, so it never needs sealing. It's one of the most chemically resistant natural stones available.

  • Daily cleaning: Soap and water — any soap, any cleaner. Soapstone is remarkably chemical-resistant.
  • Mineral oil: Apply periodically to deepen the color and create a uniform dark patina faster. This is optional — soapstone darkens naturally over time.
  • Scratches: Light scratches can be sanded out with fine-grit sandpaper and a drop of mineral oil. This is unique to soapstone — it's genuinely repairable.
  • Heat: Soapstone is extremely heat-resistant. You can set hot pots directly on it without concern.

Onyx Care

Handle with intention. Onyx is beautiful but delicate. It's best suited for low-traffic applications or accent pieces where it can shine without heavy daily use.

  • Seal every 6-12 months: Onyx is porous and needs regular sealing to resist stains
  • pH-neutral cleaners only: Acids (vinegar, citrus, wine) will etch onyx on contact
  • Avoid heavy impact: Onyx is softer than granite and quartzite — treat it gently
  • Best in low-traffic areas: Powder rooms, bar tops, accent walls — places where it's admired more than abused

What Affects the Price

Soapstone Pricing

Color, origin (Brazil is primary source), and slab size drive cost. Darker, more dramatic soapstone slabs tend to be more expensive.

Onyx Pricing

Rarity is the main factor. Backlit-suitable slabs (thin enough to transmit light) command significant premiums. Color and veining pattern also affect price.

Design Inspiration

Soapstone Applications

Farmhouse Kitchens

Soapstone's warm, matte surface is the soul of farmhouse design. Pair with apron-front sinks, open shelving, and reclaimed wood.

Wine Bars & Wet Bars

Chemical-resistant and naturally dark — soapstone hides wine stains and creates an intimate, sophisticated bar atmosphere.

Fireplace Surrounds

Soapstone has been used in wood stoves for centuries. Its heat absorption properties make it perfect for hearths and fire features.

Onyx Applications

Backlit Bar Tops

The signature onyx application. LED lighting behind translucent onyx creates a warm, ethereal glow that transforms any bar or reception area.

Powder Room Vanities

A low-traffic space where onyx can be the star. The dramatic veining turns a small room into a design moment.

Feature Walls & Art

Backlit onyx panels as wall art or accent features create gallery-worthy installations in residential and commercial spaces.

Is Soapstone or Onyx Right for You?

Soapstone

Perfect If…

  • You love the idea of a surface that ages beautifully
  • You prefer a warm, matte, tactile surface over glossy polish
  • You cook with heat and chemicals without worry
  • You appreciate farmhouse, rustic, or old-world aesthetics

Not Ideal If…

  • You want a bright, light-colored countertop (soapstone darkens)
  • You dislike the idea of visible scratches (even fixable ones)
  • You prefer a high-gloss, polished surface

Onyx

Perfect If…

  • You want a true showpiece that no one else has
  • You're designing a dramatic bar, powder room, or commercial space
  • You appreciate the artistry of backlighting natural stone

Not Ideal If…

  • You need a surface for heavy daily kitchen use
  • You want low-maintenance countertops
  • You're working with a conservative budget

Our advice: Soapstone and onyx aren't for everyone — and that's the point. If you're drawn to one of these materials, come see them in our showroom. Touching soapstone's velvety surface or seeing backlit onyx in person will tell you everything you need to know.
