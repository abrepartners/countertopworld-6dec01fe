What Is Marble?

Marble is a metamorphic rock formed when limestone is subjected to extreme heat and pressure deep within the earth. This process creates the dramatic veining and luminous depth that has made marble the premier luxury surface material for thousands of years.

Unmatched Elegance

Nothing compares to marble's visual impact. The depth, veining, and luminosity create a sense of luxury that no engineered material has fully replicated.

Natural Cool Surface

Marble stays naturally cool to the touch, making it the preferred surface for bakers and pastry chefs working with temperature-sensitive doughs.

Increases in Character

Over time, marble develops a gentle patina — a soft, lived-in quality that many homeowners find adds warmth and authenticity to their space.

Timeless Investment

Marble has graced the world's finest architecture for millennia. In a home, it signals taste and permanence in a way few other materials can match.

Premium Varieties

We source exceptional marble from Italy and around the world: Calacatta (bold, dramatic veining on warm white), Carrara (soft gray feathering — the classic), Statuario (striking gray and gold veining), Emperador (rich brown tones), and Nero Marquina (dramatic black with white veining).

Care & Maintenance

Let's be honest: Marble is a higher-maintenance surface. If you choose marble, you're choosing beauty that asks for a bit of care in return. For many homeowners, it's absolutely worth it.

Sealing

Seal marble every 6-12 months with a high-quality impregnating sealer. This is the single most important thing you can do to protect your investment. Lighter marbles may need more frequent sealing.

Daily Cleaning

Use only pH-neutral stone cleaners or very mild dish soap with warm water. Dry thoroughly after cleaning. Never let water pool on the surface.

What to Avoid

  • Acidic substances: Lemon juice, vinegar, wine, tomato sauce, and even some fruits will etch marble on contact
  • Bleach and ammonia: These damage the surface and strip sealant
  • Abrasive cleaners: Scouring powder and steel wool will scratch the polish

Etching vs. Staining

Etching (dull marks from acid contact) and staining (discoloration from absorbed liquids) are different issues. Etching affects the surface finish; staining penetrates the stone. Sealing helps prevent stains but does not prevent etching — only careful use does.

Embrace the journey: Many marble lovers view the gradual patina as part of the stone's story. Your marble countertop will tell the story of your home — every meal, every gathering, every season.

Cost Guide

Marble is a premium material, typically ranging from $75 to $250+ per square foot installed. Calacatta varieties sit at the top of the range, while Carrara offers a more accessible entry into marble luxury.

What Affects the Price

Variety & Origin

Italian Calacatta is the most expensive. Domestic and Turkish marbles offer beauty at lower price points. Rarity drives price.

Veining & Character

Dramatic, heavily-veined slabs cost more. Bookmatched marble (two slabs mirrored) commands significant premiums.

Slab Size

Larger, flawless slabs are rarer and more expensive. Smaller projects may access remnant pieces at reduced cost.

Is Marble Right for You?

Perfect For You If…

  • You appreciate natural beauty and don't mind some care
  • You view your home as an investment in lasting quality
  • You love the idea of a surface that develops character over time
  • You want a statement piece that guests will remember

Consider It If…

  • You bake frequently (marble's cool surface is ideal for pastry)
  • You're torn between marble and quartzite — visit us to compare
  • You want marble for a low-traffic area (powder room, fireplace)

Look Elsewhere If…

  • You have young kids who spill juice and leave sticky fingerprints daily
  • You cook with lots of acidic ingredients and don't want to worry
  • You want a truly zero-maintenance surface (consider engineered quartz)

Our honest advice: If you're on the fence, consider using marble in a lower-traffic area first — a bathroom vanity or a baking station. Fall in love with it there, and you'll know if it's right for your kitchen too.
